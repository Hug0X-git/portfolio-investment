02 / investor profile

Risk preference becomes a portfolio budget.

Each profile maps to lambda, target volatility, equity and crypto caps, risk budgets, and tracking error limits.

Input

Select a standard profile or apply the one inferred from the questionnaire.

Computation

Each profile defines risk aversion, exposure ceilings, and a target risk allocation by asset class.

Output

These parameters become the guardrails for the optimizer and the tactical allocation overlay.

Profile parameters

The principal values associated with the selected profile. They frame the maximum risk exposure before the asset universe is even selected.

Risk budgets by class

The risk budget indicates how much of total volatility each asset class is permitted to carry.

03 / asset universe

Core, satellite, and diversified market exposures.

The universe is filtered by data availability, annual volatility floor, and pairwise correlation redundancy before optimization.

Input

Add or remove tickers by class: equities, fixed income, commodities, real estate, and cryptocurrencies.

Filter

At runtime, assets with insufficient history, inadequate volatility, or excessive redundancy may be excluded.

Output

The final portfolio only uses valid assets aligned on the same market calendar.

04 / estimation and optimization

Robust estimates before portfolio weights.

The model includes historical returns, James-Stein shrinkage, a Black-Litterman-style market prior, Ledoit-Wolf covariance, EWMA covariance, MVO, and generalized risk budgeting.

Return

The model estimates expected returns through a conservative procedure designed to avoid overreacting to recent history.

Risk

The covariance matrix measures volatilities and correlations, which are the statistical drivers of diversification.

Weights

The optimizer combines returns, risk, and constraints to produce the strategic allocation.

07 / walk-forward backtest

Out-of-sample evidence, not hindsight.

The backtest rolls a training window forward month by month, re-estimates the model, applies weights out-of-sample, and compares against a 60/40 proxy.

Window

The model learns from a past period without observing future returns.

Test

The calculated weights are applied to the following month, after which the window advances.

Metrics

Sharpe, Sortino, drawdown, VaR, CVaR, and beta offer a fuller assessment than return alone.
